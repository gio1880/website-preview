FIRST LEGO League (FLL) Program

Empowering students through robotics, teamwork, and innovation.

Requirements

To participate in the FIRST LEGO League (FLL) program, students must meet the following requirements:

  • Age: 9 to 14 years old
  • Interest in robotics, programming, and teamwork
  • Commitment to attend weekly team meetings and practice sessions
  • Willingness to participate in regional and national competitions
  • Parental consent and support for team activities

Skills You'll Learn

By participating in the FLL program, students will develop the following skills:

Critical Thinking

Learn to analyze problems and develop innovative solutions through robotics challenges.

Teamwork

Collaborate with teammates to design, build, and program robots for competitions.

Programming

Gain hands-on experience with coding and programming robots to complete tasks.

Problem-Solving

Tackle real-world challenges by designing and testing innovative robot solutions.

Presentation Skills

Develop confidence in presenting ideas and solutions to judges and peers.

Engineering Design

Explore the engineering process by building and optimizing robot designs.

Curriculum

The FLL program focuses on teaching students critical thinking, teamwork, and robotics skills. The curriculum includes:

  • Introduction to robotics and programming
  • Team collaboration and project management
  • Research and innovation challenges
  • Robot design and performance optimization
